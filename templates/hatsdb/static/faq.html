General questions

So what is this site about?
  • This site provides access to database of TF2, Dota 2 and CS:GO items. It contains info about items and their respective owners. So if you want to find persons with unusual Team Captains or Elder Timebreakers or even Filed-Tested M4 | Asiimovs - you came to the right place. Oh yea, and there is also a server scanner.

How often do you update your database?
  • Database updates itself 24/7. Right now.

How big is the database?
  • Take a look at stats page. These numbers do not include regular weapons, ETF2L medals and other untradable/very cheap stuff.

Can I use it for free?
  • Of course you can - give it a try. Server scanner is available for free, but database access comes with some restrictions: profiles with low amount of hours (less than 300) are hidden for non-premium users.

Scanner

What features does the scanner have?
  • Scanner has:
    • Filters for untradable items and F2P players;
    • post-scan filters, such as: by name, level, quality;
    • user-defined blacklist.
    Scanner supports TF2, Dota 2 and CS:GO.
How do I scan?
  • There is a small how-to section on scanner page.
Scanner is so slow, why?
  • Scanning is performed on separate server. Let me explain how it works.
    • First of all, we send list of players to a server where it will be parsed.
    • Next, we will try to get backpack contents and if we fail - we will retry after a small delay.
    • Now we need to get amount of played hours - it takes like 70% of scanning time. Steam servers fail a lot during this procedure so there are a lot of retries.
    • After getting amount of hours we need to check every backpack for valuable items and throw away regular weapons and untradable pyrovision goggles.
    Hooray, scanning is finished. Usually it takes about 20 secs to scan 32 players, get amount of hours and perform a check for useless weapons. It can take some more time if there are a lot of users scanning at the same time - even though there is a separate server, it has its limits. Scanning can be slowed by Steam servers too - especially when they are having daily peaks of logged users (you can see it here).
